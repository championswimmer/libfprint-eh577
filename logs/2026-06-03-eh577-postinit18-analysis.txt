# EH577 post-init dump analysis

Artifacts:

- Text log: `logs/2026-06-03-eh577-postinit18-dump.txt`
- Raw responses: `dumps/2026-06-03-postinit18/`

## Summary

A full 18-packet EH575 post-init replay was captured with per-packet raw response dumps.

Key points:

- All 18 packets completed successfully.
- Packet 17 (`64 14 ec`) returned exactly `5356` bytes.
- The saved packet-17 payload is **all zero bytes**:
  - length = `5356`
  - nonzero bytes = `0`
  - unique byte values = `[0x00]`
- Response variability was observed in several smaller replies.

## Concrete response values from this capture

- `60 00 fc` -> `53 49 47 45 00 aa 01`
- `60 01 fc` -> `53 49 47 45 01 05 01`
- `60 40 fc` -> `53 49 47 45 40 80 01`
- `60 2d 02` -> `53 49 47 45 2d c7 01`
- `62 67 03` -> `53 49 47 45 67 03 01 00 ff 00`
- `60 00 02` -> `53 49 47 45 00 ab 01`

## Differences vs earlier successful post-init log

Compared with `logs/2026-06-03-eh577-auto-init-run.txt`:

- `60 2d 02` changed from `... 2d 47 01` to `... 2d c7 01`
- `62 67 03` changed from `... 67 03 01 00 00 00` to `... 67 03 01 00 ff 00`
- the large `64 14 ec` response remained a `5356`-byte zero-filled payload in both captures

## Interpretation

EH577 is clearly EH575-compatible at the framing/length level, but at least some reply bytes are stateful or sensor-dependent.

The variation is not limited to the earliest `60 00/01` state bytes; later status-like replies also change.

That suggests:

1. some command responses expose internal sensor/runtime state,
2. the future EH577 driver should preserve dynamic branching/state handling rather than hardcoding expected constants,
3. repeated controlled captures are necessary before declaring any byte positions to be fixed semantics.
