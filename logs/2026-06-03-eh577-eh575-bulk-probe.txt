# EH577 bulk probe against EH575 packet sequence

Tool: `build/eh577_usbfs_probe`
Target: `/dev/bus/usb/003/004`
Date: 2026-06-03

## Preconditions

- Device power control forced to `on`
- Device runtime status observed as `active`
- Probe used direct usbfs bulk transfers on:
  - OUT `0x01`
  - IN `0x82`

## Successful EH575-compatible exchanges

### Packet 0

TX: `45 47 49 53 60 00 fc`

RX: `53 49 47 45 00 aa 01`

Interpretation:
- `EGIS` / `SIGE` framing confirmed on EH577.
- Response shape exactly matches EH575-style 7-byte command ack.

### Packet 1

TX: `45 47 49 53 60 01 fc`

RX: `53 49 47 45 01 01 01`

Interpretation:
- This is especially interesting because the EH575 patch comments mention `01:01:01` as a state cue.
- EH577 returning the same triplet is strong evidence of protocol kinship.

### Packet 2

TX: `45 47 49 53 60 40 fc`

RX: `53 49 47 45 40 80 01`

### Packet 3

TX: `45 47 49 53 63 09 0b 83 24 00 44 0f 08 20 20 01 05 12`

RX: `53 49 47 45 09 0b 01 83 24 00 44 0f 08 20 20 01 05 12`

Interpretation:
- Longer structured command also accepted.
- Response preserves nearly all payload bytes, replacing the command byte region with what looks like an ack/status form.

### Packet 4

TX: `45 47 49 53 63 26 06 06 60 06 05 2f 06`

RX: `53 49 47 45 26 06 01 06 60 06 05 2f 06`

Interpretation:
- Another EH575 packet accepted successfully.
- Confirms EH577 is not merely recognizing the `EGIS` magic; it is understanding at least several EH575 command families.

## Important conclusion

EH577 definitely speaks an EH575-like bulk protocol on endpoints `0x01` / `0x82`.

This is the strongest result so far and materially changes the implementation strategy:

- start from the EH575 driver/prototype transport,
- not from `egis0570` or `egismoc` transport,
- then determine where EH577 diverges later in the init/capture sequence.
