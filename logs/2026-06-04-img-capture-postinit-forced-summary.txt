# EH577 img-capture post-init-forced summary

Date: 2026-06-04
Context: after patching the EH577 driver to stay on the post-init path even when `60 01 fc` returns exact `SIGE 01 01 01`.

## Result

The patched `img-capture` run no longer fails in the PRE_INIT branch.

Observed behavior:

1. driver opened successfully
2. post-init sequence completed successfully even though packet `60 01 fc` returned `SIGE 01 01 01`
3. packet `64 14 ec` completed successfully with a 5356-byte response
4. driver switched into the repeat polling loop
5. repeat packet `64 14 ec` also completed successfully with 5356-byte responses
6. in the idle run, every large frame seen so far was all-zero
7. image-device state stayed at `AWAIT_FINGER_ON`
8. no finger-present event or image submission was observed in the idle run

## Important meaning

This confirms that the earlier runtime blocker was the PRE_INIT misbranch, not a transport failure.

It also narrows the next live issue:

- EH577 can now run through post-init and repeat polling inside libfprint
- but idle polling alone does not produce a non-zero frame or finger-on transition
- this matches earlier standalone-probe evidence that meaningful EH577 data appears specifically during a correctly timed post-init finger-hold capture

## Likely next step

Run `img-capture` again with coordinated user finger timing so the sensor is already being touched during the initial post-init `64 14 ec` capture window, then inspect whether that produces non-zero data and a finger-on transition.
