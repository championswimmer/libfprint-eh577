## Summary

EH577 successfully accepted all three EH575-family bulk paths tested so far:

1. full `eh575-postinit`
2. full `eh575-preinit`
3. full `eh575-repeat`

This strengthens the conclusion that EH577 is not merely loosely similar to EH575; it is operationally compatible with the known EH575 bulk command families at least in the currently tested idle state.

## Key observations by path

### Post-init

- Accepted all 18 packets.
- `64 14 ec` returned `5356` bytes.
- Saved payload is all-zero in this idle capture.

Notable replies:

- `60 00 fc` -> `53 49 47 45 00 aa 01`
- `60 01 fc` -> `53 49 47 45 01 05 01`
- `60 40 fc` -> `53 49 47 45 40 80 01`
- `60 2d 02` -> `53 49 47 45 2d c7 01`
- `62 67 03` -> `53 49 47 45 67 03 01 00 ff 00`
- `60 0f 03` -> `53 49 47 45 0f 20 01`
- `60 00 02` -> `53 49 47 45 00 ab 01`

### Pre-init

- Accepted all 29 packets.
- The pre-init-specific `73 14 ec` did **not** return a large payload.
- Instead, `73 14 ec` returned exactly 7 zero bytes:
  - `00 00 00 00 00 00 00`

Notable replies:

- `60 00 00` -> `53 49 47 45 00 aa 01`
- `60 01 00` -> `53 49 47 45 01 05 01`
- `60 80 00` -> `53 49 47 45 80 02 01`
- `73 14 ec` -> `00 00 00 00 00 00 00`
- `60 40 ec` -> `53 49 47 45 40 00 01`
- `60 00 66` -> `53 49 47 45 00 ab 01`
- `60 01 66` -> `53 49 47 45 01 00 01`
- `60 40 66` -> `53 49 47 45 40 00 01`

Interpretation:

- EH577 accepts the EH575 pre-init packet family cleanly.
- The `73 14 ec` behavior suggests that the pre-init path is not yet the path that yields image-like bulk payloads in the tested idle state.
- The pre-init sequence also reveals additional state bytes such as `80 02 01`, `40 00 01`, and `01 00 01` that should not be assumed constant without more captures.

### Repeat

- Accepted all 9 packets.
- `64 14 ec` again returned `5356` bytes.
- Saved payload is all-zero in this idle capture.

Notable replies:

- `61 2d 20` -> `53 49 47 45 2d 20 01`
- `60 00 20` -> `53 49 47 45 00 aa 01`
- `60 01 20` -> `53 49 47 45 01 05 01`
- `60 2d 02` -> `53 49 47 45 2d c7 01`
- `62 67 03` -> `53 49 47 45 67 03 01 00 ff 00`
- `60 00 02` -> `53 49 47 45 00 aa 01`
- `64 14 ec` -> `5356` bytes, all zero

## Cross-path conclusions

1. EH577 accepts all currently tested EH575-family bulk paths.
2. Large `5356`-byte payloads have been observed on the post-init and repeat paths, but not on the tested pre-init `73 14 ec` packet.
3. All large payloads captured so far are zero-filled when idle.
4. Small reply bytes vary by command family and likely internal state.
5. Current evidence still supports an EH575-derived driver, but the EH577 state machine should remain flexible.

## Immediate follow-up implied by this comparison

1. Test the repeat path during actual finger interaction.
2. Capture non-idle `64 14 ec` payloads on post-init and repeat.
3. Check whether pre-init must be followed by some other transition before meaningful payloads appear.
4. Treat the pre-init `73 14 ec` 7-byte zero reply as a potentially important mode/state indicator.
