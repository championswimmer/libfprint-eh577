# EH577 first-two-packet state sampling

Artifacts:

- `logs/2026-06-03-eh577-first2-state-samples.txt`
- `logs/2026-06-03-eh577-first2-noreset-samples.txt`

## Summary

Two sampling series were run against the first two EH575 post-init packets:

1. 10 runs with an explicit usbfs reset before each run
2. 6 runs without resets between runs

In both series, the replies were completely stable:

- packet 0 `60 00 fc` -> `53 49 47 45 00 ab 01`
- packet 1 `60 01 fc` -> `53 49 47 45 01 00 01`

## Interpretation

This result is important because it differs from earlier successful captures that showed:

- `60 00 fc` -> `... 00 aa 01`
- `60 01 fc` -> `... 01 05 01`
- and one early one-off run with `60 01 fc` -> `... 01 01 01`

So the current evidence suggests:

1. EH577 has multiple internal states affecting at least the early status bytes.
2. Under the current reset/test conditions, the device stabilizes into an `ab` / `00` early state.
3. The earlier `aa` / `05` and `01` / `01` replies are real observations, but are not the dominant state in the current repeated sampling setup.

## Practical consequence for driver work

Do not hardcode any single early `SIGE` byte pattern as the only valid one.

The EH577 driver should tolerate at least these observed families of replies:

- `60 00 *` -> `... aa 01` or `... ab 01`
- `60 01 *` -> `... 00 01`, `... 05 01`, and previously `... 01 01`

The exact state transition conditions are still unknown.
