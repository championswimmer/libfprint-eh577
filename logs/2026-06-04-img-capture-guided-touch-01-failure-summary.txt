# EH577 img-capture guided touch 01 failure summary

Date: 2026-06-04
Context: first guided touch attempt after the post-init-forced runtime fix.

## What happened

The guided capture helper itself worked, but the capture command exited early because the EH577 dropped off the USB bus during device open.

Observed in `logs/2026-06-04-img-capture-guided-touch-01.txt`:

- libfprint successfully probed and selected the EH577 driver
- device open then failed with:
  - `libusb couldn't open USB device /dev/bus/usb/003/004, errno=5`
  - `Failed to open device: USB error on device 1c7a:0577 : Input/Output Error [-1]`

## Kernel evidence

Immediately afterward, `journalctl -k -b` showed:

- `usb 3-3: USB disconnect, device number 4`
- repeated `device descriptor read/64, error -110`
- repeated re-enumeration attempts as device numbers 8, 9, 10

## Current status

After this failure:

- `lsusb` no longer shows `1c7a:0577`
- plain libusb smoketest cannot open the EH577 either

## Conclusion

This was not primarily a bug in `tools/eh577_guided_capture.sh`.
The EH577 became unavailable at USB level during the guided `img-capture` launch, so further runtime testing is blocked until the device is recovered again.

## Next step

Recover hardware enumeration first (cold reboot / deeper power cycle), then retry with a safer guided command that performs an EH577 presence preflight before launching `img-capture`.
